3 Introduction
Range description
The arc flash detection system operates as a complement of the protection relays.
It helps to ensure rapid operation in case of arc flash incident where other
protection schemes (for example, overcurrent) operates not quick enough.
The PowerLogic A3 range is designed for the customer application of mitigating
the arc flash fault inside MV or LV cubicle and operates with the switching device
with an accessible control input.
The naming rule of PowerLogic A3 range is shown as below:

The PowerLogic A3 range is composed by A3F6P, A3F12P, A3S6P and A3S12P
to perform a high-density light detection up to 6 or 12 point-sensors per device.
ArcEM card of V321 is also released for current confirmation and light information
transmission.
The A3F series is equipped with a power supply card, therefore the A3F can
operate independently to provide arc flash protection for bay-based simple
system. Beside that, the A3F is capable to inter-operate with other PowerLogic A3
devices to form a small arc protection system for multi-bay system. It can be
associated to other PowerLogic A3 devices to increase the number of light sensor
channels and tripping outputs for multi-bay system. Depends on the number of
cubicles, the groups to be protected and the number of circuit breakers to be
controlled, the protection system designer can select up to 5 devices to build the
suitable solution.
The A3S series is not equipped with power supply card, it shall be powered by
A3F with ArcEM cable. So, it is used as only a part of arc protection system.
Both A3F and A3S series are capable to operate with V321 equipped with an
ArcEM card in slot 6. Up to 16 PowerLogic A3 devices can be connected to the
system to form a large arc protection system for the complex system.

Three kinds of operation mode
The PowerLogic A3 brings a certain flexibility to fit to different customer
applications. The modular design of the PowerLogic A3 allows the user to design
an arc flash protection solution adapted to the installation design and protection
objectives.
For the simple power system, single PowerLogic A3 device can meet the
requirement. For a complex power system, the arc protection system shall be
formed by multiple devices. Furthermore, PowerLogic A3 can be connected to
V321 through its ArcEM card in slot 6. In this case, up to 16 PowerLogic A3
devices cascaded with V321 build up a large arc protection system for multiple
sections busbar, multiple incomers, and couplings power system.
Using a single PowerLogic A3 device or combining multiple units depends on the
factors below:
•

The number of groups (which is exactly the same concept as Zone 1 to 4 in
Schneider Electric‘s legacy products, for example VAM10L, VAM12L) to
protect;

•

The number of sensors to deploy;

•

The number of output relays to trip the circuit breaker;

•

The application scenarios: selectivity/non-selectivity, with/without current
confirmation, incomer/feeder.

According to the scale of the arc protection system which is composed by the
PowerLogic A3 devices, three kinds of operation mode is defined as below:

14

•

Stand-alone mode: also called single unit mode, full size PowerLogic A3
device equipped with a power supply card operates independently, no
communication function.

•

System mode: not more than 5 PowerLogic A3 devices form a small system.
The system is powered by A3F devices, the A3S devices are powered by
ArcEM cable. Internal messages containing status of Groups, reset and install
commands are transmitted by the ArcEM bus.

•

I/O peripheral mode: builds up an arc protection system with V321 device, as
an alternative of some Schneider Electric products such as VAM10L, VAM12L
and so on with more flexible and versatile to meet various application
scenarios.

System mode
An arc protection system of not more than 5 PowerLogic A3 devices can be built
up by connecting the PowerLogic A3 devices with dedicated ArcEM cables. This
arc protection system contains maximum 60 sensors and 10 output relays, can be
used in variant applications.
There are some limitations to pay attention of:
•

Each A3S shall be cascaded to a A3F device considering the limited capacity
of power card in A3F. It means the cascade rule is -F-S-F-S-, S can’t be
connected to S. So, at least 2 full size PowerLogic A3 devices are needed
when the system scale is 5 or 3.

•

The total cable length in the system shall be less than 100 m and the maximum
length between two adjacent PowerLogic A3 devices is 30 m.

The protection scheme follows these rules:
•

There are 3 elements in each protection scheme logic diagram: the inputs, the
logic and the outputs

•

The inputs include all sensors either local or remote and current condition (if
any, according to configured scheme). For example, in the above image, S1 to
S6 are used as local sensors. Light group is used as remote sensors from
PowerLogic A3 devices and current confirmation condition is used as well.
Inputs data flow is defined by “Scheme” rotary switch and “Light Group” rotary
switch.

•

The outputs include tripping outputs either HSO (T1 &T3) or conventional (T2)
and light group which is shared as remote sensors data on the communication
bus. Output data acquisition is defined by “Scheme” rotary switch and “Light
Group” rotary switch.

•

Each PowerLogic A3 device can receive signal from the remote sensors
because the signal is published as generic event. The restriction is each
PowerLogic A3 can receive this data and execute it in its logic (Scheme) only
in case of the “Light Group” setting of the PowerLogic A3 is identical with the
sender device. It means the sender and the receiver must have same Light
Group settings.

Supporting functions
Test mode
The test mode is used to verify the sensor reliability and the system configuration
during commissioning or maintenance. In test mode, the device keeps operate
according to setting parameters and inputs, but the physical output relays will be
blocked. For example, an A3F6P works on Scheme#4, Group 2 enters test mode.
Activate the sensor 3, the LEDs of sensor 3 and T2 will light up but physical T2
output relay will not trip. Group 2 message in ArcEM bus also can be grabbed, T2
LED light up without physical T2 output relay trip. Therefore, the consistency
between the wiring of sensors and the parameter settings of devices can be
directly checked visually by LEDs. Also, the bit ‘simulation’ of Groups messages
transmitted through the ArcEM bus will be set to ON. Therefore, the test mode is
accessible not only for individual device, but also for PowerLogic A3 system.
NOTE: The V321 device is without test mode, but the bit ‘simulation’ of Group
messages transmitted through the ArcEM bus will be checked before taking
actions. Therefore, the sensor of PowerLogic A3 device which is in test mode
cannot activate the arc stages in V321.

Introduction of configuration methods
As introduced in the chapters Introduction, page 11 and Use, page 21, the
operation of PowerLogic A3 requires mainly the configuration of the following
parameters and commands:
•

Protection scheme

•

Light group

•

Current group

•

Output relay latch or not

•

Install/Reset command

According to the application scenarios of PowerLogic A3, there are three methods
to configure the PowerLogic A3 device:
•

By rotary switch and push button: It is the method for a single PowerLogic
A3 device or a small system as it is quite easy and convenient. The protection
scheme and light group depends on the position of rotary switch. The current
group is set to default value Group 16. The output relays are in latch mode.
Users can implement Install/Reset command by push button in full size
PowerLogic A3 device.

•

By configure tool eSetup Easergy Pro directly: The dedicated configure
cable REL52960 which converts the ix Industrial® ports to RJ45 is a mandatory
accessory. This method uses Ethernet IP address. All parameters and
commands can be configured by the eSetup Easergy Pro. The other settings
and functions such as device name edit and force outputs are also supported.

•

By ArcEM Unit Configurator of V321: In this method, PowerLogic A3 works
in I/O peripheral mode, it means the V321 is in the system. The PowerLogic A3
device can be configured through ArcEM configurator embedded in V321 by
the front USB port. All parameters and commands are recommended to be
configured by V321.

Configure with rotary switch and push button
There are two rotary switches on each PowerLogic A3 device. They are used to
set the parameters of protection scheme and light group. Meanwhile, there is a
push button on the full size PowerLogic A3 device to implement the reset and
install command. With these two physical interfaces, users can complete the
configuration of single PowerLogic A3 device or PowerLogic A3 small system. The
current group is set to default value Group 16. The output relays are latch mode.

it means the device or system is installed successfully.

install failed. ON/C/E LED will be in red and T4 will trip. If everything is running
normally, only the ON/C/E LED will light up in green.

9Maintenance

9 Maintenance
DANGER
HAZARD OF UNEXPECTED SYSTEM OPERATION
•

If the arc flash detection unit is no longer supplied with power or is in
permanent non-operational state, the protection functions are no longer
active and all the output contacts are dropped out.

•

To detect a power-off or a permanent error state, connect the watchdog
output contact to a monitoring device such as SCADA or DCS.

Failure to follow these instructions will result in death or serious injury.
The PowerLogic A3 requires maintenance to help ensure that it works according
to the specification. Keep record of the maintenance actions performed for the
system. The maintenance can include, but is not limited to, the following actions.

Preventative maintenance
The PowerLogic A3 device, sensor and cabling shall be visually checked when the
switchgear is de-energized. During such inspection pay attention to
•

possible dirty arc sensors

•

loose wire connections

•

damaged wiring

•

indicator lights

•

other mechanical connections

Visual inspection shall be made minimum every three (3) years.

Periodical testing
The PowerLogic A3 device, cabling and sensors must periodically be tested
according to the end-user's safety instructions, national safety instructions or law.
Manufacturer recommend functional testing being carried minimum every five (5)
years.
It is proposed that the periodic testing is conducted with a secondary injection
principle for those protection stages which are used in the IED and its related
units.

Hardware cleaning
Pay special attention to help to ensure that the device, its extension units and
sensors are always clean.

DANGER
HAZARD OF UNEXPECTED SYSTEM OPERATION
•

Do not use any type of solvents or gasoline to clean the device, sensors or
cables.

•

When cleaning the sensor, make sure that the cleaning solution does not
contact anything other than the sensor.

Failure to follow these instructions will result in death or serious injury.
